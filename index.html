Carl Sagan

A bright light that guides us in the darkness

Time line of Carl Sagan life's on this Pale Blue Dot

  • 1934-Carl Sagan was born on November 9, 1934, in Brooklyn, New York City
  • 1939-He showed an early interest in science, particularly astronomy, inspired by visits to the 1939 World's Fair
  • 1951-Sagan attended the University of Chicago, earning degrees in physics, astronomy, and astrophysics
  • 1955-He received a Ph.D. in astronomy and astrophysics from the University of Chicago, focusing on planetary atmospheres
  • 1959-Sagan began his career at prestigious institutions like Harvard University and the Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory
  • 1960s-He played significant roles in NASA missions, including Mariner, Viking, Voyager, and Galileo spacecraft
  • 1980-Sagan's popular science book, "Cosmos: A Personal Voyage," accompanied the influential television series of the same name, reaching millions worldwide
  • 1980-Sagan received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to science and education, including the Pulitzer Prize for his book "The Dragons of Eden"
  • 1996-He passed away on December 20, leaving behind a legacy of scientific discovery, education, and advocacy for the wonders of the universe
  • present-His influence extends beyond academia, shaping public perception of science and fostering a deeper appreciation for the cosmos and our place within it
